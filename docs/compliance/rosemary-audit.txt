Data Protection Law · NDPA 2023 · GAID 2025

NDPA / GAID Compliance Review — NDA Document
Document Reviewed: Non-Disclosure Agreement (Signova Generated)
Framework: Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 & GAID 2025

This Non-Disclosure Agreement provides a structured framework and includes references to NDPA compliance.

However, from a data protection and audit perspective, the document currently reflects baseline compliance awareness, not operational compliance readiness.

Key gaps exist in:
- lawful basis definition
- data lifecycle management
- enforceability of obligations
- the absence of Data Processing Agreement provisions
- inadequate cross-border transfer framework

These gaps could expose users to compliance risks in real-world implementation. These deficiencies are not technical oversights — they represent fundamental gaps in the data protection architecture of the agreement that expose both parties to regulatory penalty under Section 48 NDPA 2023 of up to ₦10,000,000 or 2% of annual gross revenue, whichever is greater.

Key Compliance Gaps & Recommendations

2.1 Lawful Basis for Processing
Observation: The agreement defines personal data but does not specify the lawful basis for processing.
Risk: Processing personal data without a defined lawful basis is non-compliant under NDPA and would not withstand regulatory review.
Recommendation:
- Expand personal data definition to include full Section 65 NDPA 2023 definition and sensitive data category
- Include a clause requiring each party to establish and document a valid lawful basis (e.g., consent, contract, legitimate interest) before processing personal data.

2.2 Data Minimisation
Observation: There is no restriction on the scope of personal data that may be shared.
Risk: Over-collection of data increases exposure and violates NDPA principles.
Recommendation: Add a clause limiting data sharing to only what is necessary for the defined purpose.

2.3 Data Retention & Deletion
Observation: The NDA does not define retention timelines or deletion obligations.
Risk: Indefinite data retention is a key compliance failure under GAID 2025.
Recommendation: Include clear provisions requiring:
- defined retention periods
- secure deletion or anonymization after purpose is fulfilled

2.4 Security Measures (Technical & Organisational)
Observation: The clause references "appropriate security measures" but lacks specificity.
Risk: Vague security language does not meet regulatory expectations.
Recommendation: Strengthen to include:
- encryption
- access controls
- internal data governance processes

2.5 Data Subject Rights
Observation: No provision addressing data subject rights.
Risk: Failure to support rights (access, rectification, erasure) creates direct NDPA exposure.
Recommendation: Include a clause requiring mechanisms to support data subject rights.

2.6 Audit & Compliance Verification
Observation: No audit or verification rights are included.
Risk: Lack of oversight reduces enforceability, especially in vendor relationships.
Recommendation: Include a clause allowing parties to request evidence of compliance or conduct audits where necessary.

2.7 Cross-Border Data Transfer
Observation: Clause exists but is high-level and non-specific.
Risk: Does not fully address GAID 2025 safeguards for international transfers.
Recommendation: Expand to include:
- approved transfer mechanisms
- contractual safeguards
- adequacy considerations

2.8 Breach Notification Structure
Observation: 72-hour notification is included (aligned with NDPA).
Gap: No clear internal notification hierarchy between parties.
Recommendation: Clarify immediate notification to the other party before regulatory escalation.

2.9 Duration of Confidentiality
Observation: Confidentiality period non-existent.
Risk: May be insufficient for personal data obligations.
Recommendation: Specify that data protection obligations survive termination where applicable.

3. Strategic Insight
The document demonstrates strong legal drafting capability, but like most generated agreements, it operates at a template level.

The key compliance risk for users is not the absence of documentation but the gap between what is documented and how data is actually handled in practice.

Update compliance clause to explicitly reference GAID 2025 as primary binding directive.

4. Conclusion
This NDA can be strengthened into an audit-ready document with targeted improvements.

More importantly, aligning these clauses with actual data flows and operational practices will be critical to ensuring real compliance under NDPA and GAID 2025.
